Unmanned Aerial Fire-Fighting Assistant

University of British Columbia

Summary

For my capstone project at UBC, my team's objective was to design a robust and autonomous tether connection from drone to tank that can carry heavy payloads and demonstrate electrical connection capability via an LED light. Aerial firerighting is essential for combating the increasing number of wildfires around the world. Autonomous drones can reduce costs and enhance crew safety by operating in larger numbers and at night.

The picture on the left shows the black 3D printed hook which supports the paylod. The round magnetic plug is attached to a linear actuator that extends after the pin locking mechanism engages.

What?
  • Design a robust and autonomous tether connection from drone to tank that can carry heavy payloads and demonstrate electrical connection capability
  • Must support 3000 lb, connect at 30° angles, withstand 150 °C, and resist dirt and water
  • The 2 main evaluation criteria were consistency and connection speed.
How?
  • Designed on SolidWorks
  • The initial prototype was constructed from wood to explore feasibility and identify areas for improvement
  • The mechanical hook latches onto the D-ring and locks in place with a pin. An actuator then extends to connect the magnetic electrical plug, establishing the electrical connection. The entire system is enclosed for protection against heat and dirt
Results
  • FEA included pin shear and actuator frame buckling under 3 kN load; redesigned for margin > 2.5 with reduced mass
  • Average connection time of 10 seconds with an average consistency of 9.3/10
  • Electrical connection demonstrated via LED light
  • Environmental robustness requirements met
  • The sponsors (Cascade Aerospace) were very happy with the design
